## How We Tested

Our testing panel included two licensed physiotherapists, a certified athletic trainer, and three recreational athletes. Over 12 weeks, we evaluated 19 massage guns using the following criteria:

- **Stall force** — measured with a digital force gauge at each speed setting
- **Noise level** — recorded in decibels at 12 inches using a calibrated sound meter
- **Battery life** — timed from full charge to shutoff at medium speed
- **Ergonomics** — rated by each tester on a 1–10 scale for reach, grip comfort, and weight balance
- **Build quality** — assessed through drop tests, attachment durability, and long-term motor performance
- **Recovery effectiveness** — subjective soreness reduction scores after standardized post-workout routines

Each device went through a minimum of 40 hours of use before final scoring.

## Buying Guide: How to Choose a Massage Gun

### Stall Force

Stall force measures how much pressure you can apply before the motor stops. Higher stall force (50+ lbs) is essential for larger athletes or anyone working on dense muscle groups like glutes and quads. Casual users can get effective relief with 30–40 lbs.

### Amplitude

Amplitude is the depth of each percussion stroke, measured in millimeters. Devices with 14–16 mm amplitude reach deeper tissue layers, while 10–12 mm devices work closer to the surface. Deeper is not always better — a 12 mm device at high speed can feel aggressive on sensitive areas like the neck and forearms.

### Noise Level

Massage gun noise ranges from roughly 50 dB (quiet conversation) to 75 dB (vacuum cleaner). If you plan to use the device around others, at work, or while watching TV, prioritize models under 60 dB at their lowest setting.

### Battery Life

Most massage guns last 90–300 minutes on a full charge. Consider how often you want to recharge. Devices like the Ekrin B37 (480 minutes) need charging far less frequently than the Theragun Pro (150 minutes per battery). Swappable batteries, like those on the Theragun, let you carry extras for extended sessions.

### Weight and Ergonomics

A massage gun that tires your arm out before your muscles feel better defeats the purpose. Devices under 2.5 lbs are easier to maneuver, especially when reaching your back and shoulders. Angled or adjustable handles (like the Theragun's rotating arm) significantly reduce wrist fatigue during extended sessions.

### Attachments

Most devices include 4–6 heads. The most versatile set includes:

- **Ball** — general purpose for large muscle groups
- **Flat** — broad, firm pressure across wide areas
- **Bullet** — targeted trigger point and knot work
- **Fork** — designed for the spine and Achilles tendon
- **Dampener/cushion** — softer head for bony or sensitive areas

### Warranty

Warranties range from 1 year to lifetime. A longer warranty reflects manufacturer confidence and protects your investment, which matters given these devices contain high-RPM motors subject to wear.

## FAQ

### How often should you use a massage gun?

Most sports physiotherapists recommend using a massage gun for 1–2 minutes per muscle group, up to 15 minutes total per session. You can safely use one daily for post-workout recovery or general tension relief. Avoid prolonged use on a single spot, as excessive percussion can bruise tissue or cause inflammation.

### Can a massage gun replace a deep tissue massage?

A massage gun can replicate some benefits of a professional massage — increased blood flow, reduced muscle tension, and faster recovery — but it cannot replace the skilled assessment and adaptive technique of a licensed therapist. Think of it as an effective daily maintenance tool between professional sessions, not a complete substitute.

### Are massage guns safe for everyone?

Most healthy adults can safely use a massage gun. However, you should avoid percussive therapy if you have blood clotting disorders, deep vein thrombosis, severe varicose veins, or open wounds in the treatment area. People with nerve sensitivity conditions, osteoporosis, or recent fractures should consult their physician first. Never use a massage gun directly on the spine, joints, or bony prominences.

### What is the difference between amplitude and percussions per minute?

Amplitude measures how far the massage head travels on each stroke (in millimeters), which determines how deeply the percussion reaches into tissue. Percussions per minute (PPM) measures speed — how many strokes occur per minute. High amplitude with moderate PPM produces deep, forceful strokes. Low amplitude with high PPM produces fast, surface-level vibration. For deep tissue work, prioritize amplitude (14–16 mm). For gentle relaxation, a lower amplitude at moderate PPM works well.

### Is a more expensive massage gun always better?

Not necessarily. Our testing showed that the Ekrin B37 at $230 outperformed several devices priced above $300 in stall force and battery life. Premium devices like the Theragun Pro justify their cost through ergonomic design, app integration, and build quality — features that matter to some users and not others. Start by identifying which specifications matter most to you, then compare devices in your budget range.

### Should I use a massage gun before or after a workout?

Both. Before a workout, use a massage gun at a low-to-medium setting for 30–60 seconds per muscle group to increase blood flow and prime tissue for activity. After a workout, use a medium-to-high setting for 1–2 minutes per muscle group to reduce soreness and accelerate recovery. Research published in the *Journal of Clinical and Diagnostic Research* found that percussive therapy applied post-exercise significantly reduced delayed-onset muscle soreness (DOMS) at 24, 48, and 72 hours compared to a control group.

### How long do massage gun batteries last before needing replacement?

Lithium-ion batteries in massage guns typically retain 80% of their original capacity after 300–500 full charge cycles. For daily users, this means roughly 1–2 years before you notice shorter runtime. Devices with replaceable batteries (like the Theragun Pro) let you swap in a fresh pack without servicing the entire unit. Non-removable batteries may eventually require manufacturer repair or replacement.
